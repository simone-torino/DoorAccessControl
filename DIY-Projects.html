Simone Torino

During my studies I had to start by the basics and build up from there my own practical skills, like designing and building electronic circuits and, most importantly, making them do something useful. Carrying forward these projects has made me develop strong autonomy in problem solving and it taught me to always stay focused on what the final result needs to look like.

High Voltage detector

  • Analog circuit used to detect mains voltage without contact
  • Made of a Darlington pair that amplifies the signal coming from the aluminum foil "antenna"
  • When a live wire is near the white LED starts blinking.

Automatic Watering

  • Simple setup to periodically activate a 5V water pump
  • Based on a STM32 programmed with HAL functions
  • A Darlington pair drives the current to the pump, taking the signal from a digital output of the microcontroller
