In the first year pursuing my Electronic Engineering Master's degree at Polytechnic of Turin I had the opportunity to join two very interesting group projects, that were awared with extra marks on the relative exam. Delivering university projects has taught me how to collaborate effectively with my peers even if I didn't know them before and the importance of time management when the team has to meet a deadline.

Pong game on FPGA

  • Written in VHDL on the VGA interface of a Intel Cyclone V FPGA
  • Up to three different game modes
  • Scores are shown in real time on the FPGA's integrated display
  • Supports multiplayer 
Repository on Github

Access Control with Raspberry Pi

  • Intuitive user interface made on a Pi 4 and based on RFID.
  • Registered users can be managed from a website made with  Flask, Flutter, MySQL and Nginx.
  • Supports user registration through secure OTP.
Repository on Github
